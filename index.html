Who is Billie Jean King?

Billie Jean King is a legendary American former World No. 1 professional tennis player. She is widely regarded as one of the greatest tennis players of all time and a champion for gender equality both on and off the court.

Here are some key things to know about her:

*   **Tennis Career:** King won 39 Grand Slam titles: 12 in singles, 16 in women's doubles, and 11 in mixed doubles. She was a dominant force in tennis during the 1960s and 1970s.
*   **"Battle of the Sexes":** King is perhaps most famous for her 1973 match against Bobby Riggs, a retired male tennis player who claimed that women's tennis was inferior. King's victory in this highly publicized event was a major moment for women's sports and the feminist movement.
*   **Gender Equality Advocate:** She has been a lifelong advocate for gender equality in sports and society. She founded the Women's Tennis Association (WTA) and the Women's Sports Foundation.
*   **Activism:** King has been a vocal advocate for LGBTQ+ rights and has used her platform to promote inclusion and acceptance.
*   **Legacy:** Her impact extends far beyond tennis. She is a cultural icon and an inspiration to athletes and activists worldwide. She has received numerous awards and honors, including the Presidential Medal of Freedom.

In short, Billie Jean King is a tennis legend, a pioneer for women's equality, and a champion for social justice.
